Biography

I am a Research Assistant at Ålands fredsinstitut in Mariehamn, where I support research on Åland's demilitarisation and neutralisation as well as education relating to territorial autonomy and the Åland example.

I completed my MSSc in Peace & Conflict Studies at Uppsala University in 2025 as a Rotary Peace Fellow. My thesis examined gender inequality as a moderator of the relationship between climate change and communal violence in sub-Saharan Africa. Prior to my master's, I worked for nearly a decade in the peacebuilding sector in Washington, DC—most recently as Programs & Networks Officer at Search for Common Ground, co-leading ConnexUs, a global knowledge-sharing platform serving 430,000+ users.

Publications
Willoughby, Z. (2025)
Saving Strangers or Keeping Ourselves Safe? An Analysis of Peacekeeping & Unarmed Civilian Protection
Pax et Bellum Journal, 12. doi:10.33063/pbj.v12i2025.702
Examines the roles, strengths, and limitations of UN peacekeeping and unarmed civilian protection (UCP) as tools in conflict resolution. Using a case study in Bentiu, South Sudan, the paper reveals how UCP strategies—including Women's Protection Teams—address protection gaps left by peacekeepers, and argues for a nuanced integration of the two approaches centred on community agency and relational R2P.

Willoughby, Z. (2025)
Boiling Points: Gender Inequality as a Moderator for Climate Change & Communal Violence
Master's thesis, Department of Peace and Conflict Research, Uppsala University. Supervised by Sebastian van Baalen.
Quantitative analysis of whether gender inequality moderates the relationship between climate variability and communal violence in sub-Saharan Africa, combining SPEI drought indices with subnational conflict and gender inequality datasets.
